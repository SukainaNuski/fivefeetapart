Introduction

Five Feet Apart is a 2019 American romantic drama film directed by Justin Baldoni (in his directorial debut) and written by Rachael Lippincott with Mikki Daughtry and Tobias Iaconis. The film was inspired by Claire Wineland, who suffered from cystic fibrosis. Haley Lu Richardson and Cole Sprouse play two young patients with cystic fibrosis who try to have a relationship despite being forced to stay six feet apart from each other.

Main Characters

Stella Grant

A high school senior living with cystic fibrosis and one of the protagonists of the novel. Diagnosed with cystic fibrosis at the age of six, Stella is extremely rules-oriented and strict about her regimen to keep herself healthy to qualify for a lung transplant. While realistic and sardonic about her illness, Stella is willing to do whatever it takes to stay alive for as long as possible, especially since her parents divorced after the death of her sister Abby. Stella knows how to code and built an app to set custom reminders for people living with chronic illnesses. Despite her initial dislike of Will and the dangers of being physically near him, Stella falls in love with Will

William Newman

A teenager living with cystic fibrosis and one of the protagonists of the novel. Will has a fatal complication called B. cepacia and has been receiving experimental treatments all over the world for the past eight months in spite of his objections. Will believes that since his death is inevitable, he should not spend what time he has left cooped up in a hospital. Will has dreams to travel the world and is a cartoonist.

Poe Ramirez

A teenager living with cystic fibrosis and Stella’s best friend. Poe and Stella met in the hospital as children and have supported each other ever since. Because Poe’s parents were deported to Colombia, Poe is a ward of the state until he turns eighteen. Poe has dreams of being a chef and enjoys skateboarding and soccer.

Plot

Stella Grant and Will Newman both have cystic fibrosis (CF) and must stay six feet apart due to their compromised immune systems. Stella is meticulous about her treatments, while Will, who is part of a drug trial and infected with B. cepacia, is more rebellious. As they bond over their shared struggles, their relationship deepens into love.


Despite their feelings, Stella’s G-tube infection requires surgery, and she faces it alone after learning her sister Abby died a year ago. Will supports her by singing Abby’s song. Later, nurse Barb warns Will about the dangers of breaking the six-foot rule. Realizing the risk, Will decides to end their relationship to protect Stella.


On Will's birthday, after Poe, Stella's CF friend, dies, Stella and Will leave the hospital to see lights she’s always dreamed of. Stella falls through ice but is saved by Will, who performs CPR despite the infection risk. Stella survives and undergoes a successful lung transplant. Will, knowing he might have infected her, says a final goodbye, leaving her his sketchbook of drawings.

Release

The novel was published in November 2018 which allowed printing to begin in February of the following year. The first print run consisted of 90,000. These copies were used to promote the upcoming release of the film.

The film was released in the United States on March 15, 2019 by CBS Films via Lionsgate.
